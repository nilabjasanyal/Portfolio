Color Quell

About 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women have some form of color blindness. Color quell is a tool that can simulate the visual experience of color blindness for people with normal color vision. This tool can be used by designers, developers, and others who work with visual content to better understand how their work may appear to people with different types of color vision deficiencies.

Hand-Swipe

The conventional techniques for managing a PowerPoint presentation might be constrained and call for the usage of tangible objects like a keyboard or mouse. Additionally, it can be difficult and time-consuming to draw or annotate while giving a presentation. The goal of this project is to get over these restrictions by creating a system that makes it possible to control a PowerPoint presentation with hand gestures.
